    Course Description
    ðŸ”—
    Applies advanced mathematics to problems of science. Identifies the three phases of such an application and develops skills necessary to carry out each step: a) translation of the given physical information into a mathematical model; b) treatment of the model by mathematical methods; c) interpretation of the mathematical result in physical terms. Investigates topics including vector analysis, Taylor and Fourier series, partial differential equations and their applications.

    Attendance.

    Attendance and class participation are probably the single most important factors in your success in this course. The policy for this course is that students enrolled in MATH 342 take responsibility for their own learning and attend class regularly. There will be occasional in-class activities that are completed and submitted during class. These activities are a graded component of the course, and you must be present to earn credit.

    Late work.

    For homework, late submissions are allowed with a 25% penalty per day. For in-class activities and quizzes, make-ups are allowed for individual work, but they must be completed before the next class period. No make-ups are allowed for group work. For exams, you must contact the instructor by email within 24 hours of missing an exam and be prepared to provide documentation.
