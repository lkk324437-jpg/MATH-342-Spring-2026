    Definition 1. Taylor Series.

    The Taylor series expansion, centered at a point \(a\text{,}\) of a function \(f(x)\) is
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=f(a)+\frac{f'(a)}{1!}(x-a)+\frac{f''(a)}{2!}(x-a)^2+\frac{f'''(a)}{3!}(x-a)^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
    Let \(h=x-a\text{,}\) then the previous equation becomes
    \begin{equation*} f(x)=f(a)+\frac{f'(a)}{1!}h+\frac{f''(a)}{2!}h^2+\frac{f'''(a)}{3!}h^3+\cdots \end{equation*}
    🔗
    🔗

    Remark 2.

    The commonly used format of Taylor series is
    \begin{equation} f(x+h)=f(x)+f'(x)h+\frac{f''(x)}{2!}h^2+\frac{f'''(x)}{3!}h^3+\cdots\tag{1} \end{equation}
    If \(h\) is small, the terms involving \(h^4\text{,}\) \(h^5, \ldots\) can be ignored (assuming \(|f^{(n)}(x)|\) is finite.)
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 3. Finite Difference Methods.

    From (1) we can derive the following approximations:
    • Forward Difference: \(f'(x)\approx \frac{1}{h}\left[f(x+h)-f(x)\right]\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • Backward Difference: \(f'(x)\approx \frac{1}{h}\left[f(x)-f(x-h)\right]\)
      🔗
      🔗
    • Central Difference for 1st order derivative:
      \begin{equation} f'(x)\approx \frac{1}{2h}\left[f(x+h)-f(x-h)\right]\tag{2} \end{equation}
      🔗
      🔗
    • Central Difference for 2nd order derivative:
      \begin{equation} f''(x)\approx \frac{1}{h^2}\left[f(x+h)-2f(x)+f(x-h)\right]\tag{3} \end{equation}
      🔗
      🔗
    This process is also called discretization since we focus on finding values of the solution on discrete points, instead of an explicit formula of the entire domain. This often provides more flexibility when simulating more realistic scenario.
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 6. Laplace’s Equation.

    \begin{equation} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}+\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2}=0\tag{4} \end{equation}
    🔗
    🔗

    Definition 8. Central Difference in 2D.

    \begin{align} \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} \amp\approx \frac{1}{h^2}\left[u(x+h,y)-2u(x,y)+u(x-h,y)\right]\tag{5}\\ \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} \amp\approx \frac{1}{h^2}\left[u(x,y+h)-2u(x,y)+u(x,y-h)\right]\tag{6} \end{align}
    🔗
    🔗

    Remark 9.

    At every point \(P_{ij}=(x_i,y_j)\text{,}\) we can approximate the Laplace’s Equation (4) using central difference (5-point approximation):
    \begin{align*} \amp \frac{\partial^2 u(x_i,y_j)}{\partial x^2}+\frac{\partial^2 u(x_i,y_j)}{\partial y_j} \\ \approx \amp \frac{1}{h^2}\left[u(x_{i+1},y_j)-2u(x_i,y_j)+u(x_{i-1},y_j)\right] \\ \amp +\frac{1}{h^2}\left[u(x_i,y_{j+1})-2u(x_i,y_j)+u(x_i,y_{j-1})\right] \end{align*}
    For simplicity, we denote \(u(x_i,y_j)=u_{i,j}\text{.}\) Then we can rewrite (4) at \((x_i,y_j)\) as
    \begin{equation*} \frac{1}{h^2}\left[u_{i+1,j}+u_{i-1,j}+u_{i,j+1}+u_{i,j-1}-4u_{i,j}\right]=0 \end{equation*}
